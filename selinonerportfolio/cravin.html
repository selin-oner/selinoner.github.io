Project Overview

Cravin is a mobile food app that helps users find, order, and pick up food on the road quicker. We prioritize convenience for people who are always on the go. Simply put in your destination, select a restaurant from the various options, and order! Get your food with no waiting time and continue on your way. The app recommends restaurants en route to any predetermined destination and calculates the drive time and food preparation time so that your food is ready right when you arrive.

The Process

→ UX Research
Cravin App
Collaborating with two developers and a project manager, I used our previous ideas to start UX research for the Cravin App. I first looked into our competitors' UI and noted down similarities, as well as how Cravin could have a different approach to a similar feature. Afterwards, I downloaded existing food delivery applications to see their UX and user journeys.
→ Low-Fidelity Model
Cravin App
Following the UX research, with my team, I discussed which features out of my notes would be included in the initial lo-fi design. I made multiple designs of every component to see how they would all match up together and whether or not they were a good fit for the Cravin app.
→ High-Fidelity Model
Cravin App
To have a prototype ready for our presentations and investor meetings, I got designed the high-fi model.
→ High-Fidelity Model
Cravin Website
After designing an initial prototype, I then worked on our website. I decided on which information would be included for both the customers and the restaurants. The website was more like a platform for interested users and restaurants to join our waitlist easily. I continued with our bright yellow theme and made small graphics to make the website more appealing.
→ Brainstorming
Restaurant App
This new app for Cravin would be for the partner restaurants to use. I brainstormed ideas and possible pages that would be included in the Restaurant-Side App.
→ UX Research
Restaurant App
Adding onto our brainstorming, I went into more depth by researching other specific UX components used in similar companies' apps. I also made a pro-con list to decide whether the platform for restaurant management would be a website or a mobile application.
→ Low-Fidelity Model
Restaurant App
After extensive research (and deciding on making an app instead of a website), I got started on the lo-fi version of the Cravin Restaurant App. I mapped out and added all the components that would be included in the final version of the app.
→ Promotional Materials
Cravin App
I've made our business cards and brochures to give out to possible partner restaurants. Additionally, I've made another version of the brochure that would have general information of the company for possible investors.

Key Takeaways

1. Organize your ideas.
I had a lot of creative liberty when it came to this project, so I had a lot of ideas. It was crucial for me to arrange them to have an end product that was clear and concise.
2. Get creative.
When it comes to your own startup, it's important to brainstorm and come up with different ideas that would represent the brand best.
3. Learn from each other.
Working in a team where each person has a different expertise allowed me to get feedback from various perspectives.
